## Manifesto (from the process book ending statement)

Urban gardening should be the ultimate combination of technology and nature. Those in the bustling city should have the ability to care for plants despite busy scheduling. That's what the products from Aeroway seek to solve.

## Features (anatomy)

1. AIR DRAFT PANEL — Painted aluminum sheet-metal grates with an activated carbon panel behind them. The carbon panel lifts out from the top for easy replacement.
2. PLANT HOLDERS — PVC pipes set at a 45-degree angle. Plant roots hang inside the tower, bathed in the nutrient film.
3. HOSE + SPINE — The hose connects to the water pump inside the bucket. The spine secures the hose and continues the tower's form.
4. PUMP — A SeaFlow 10ft pump hidden inside the bucket. The power cord loops through a hole in the back.
5. AIR INTAKE — Circular intake modeled on standard air filter geometry, integrated seamlessly into the base.
6. BASE PEGS — Lock the bucket in for stability and security. 0.1mm fillets for a tight fit; the longer rear peg completes the continuous form.

## Materials

Tower + holders: HDPE plastic · Filter housing: painted aluminum · Ports: PVC · Reservoir: 2.5 gal bucket

## How it works (6 steps)

1. ADD WATER — Fill the reservoir bucket.
2. SET THE BASE — Place the bucket on the base and connect the hose.
3. MOUNT THE TOWER — Lower the tower onto the base.
4. POWER ON — Connect the hose to the tower, plug in. Water flow activates.
5. PLANT — Place plants in the pipes and add nutrients.
6. LET IT RUN — The tower grows your plants and filters your air. Hands free.

## Design process story

Concepts explored: Root-Bed, Water Grinder, Smart Planter, Smart Planter 2, Nu Grow, NuTree, AeroWay, Canopy.

Two finalists merged into one product:

CANOPY (a self-sustaining street lamp nurturing living trees through an internal hydroponic core) contributed the sleek towering form.

AEROWAY (a home planter and air purifier that takes in contaminated air and returns clean oxygen) contributed the function.

Form development: foam models tested tower form, exterior opening, air filter form, and base form.

Validation findings — CLARITY: purpose must be obvious, clear symbols, hidden mechanics. AESTHETICS: minimalist, softer rounded forms, room for personalization. USABILITY: hands-off and low-effort wins; size and scale matter.

## Prototypes

V1 "BUCKET TOWER" — Built from a DIY tutorial at 4 feet tall. Proved the concept and proved the size wrong: it took up too much space. This set the desk-scale constraint.

FINAL PROTOTYPE — Canopy's form, AeroWay's function, scaled to 2 feet for the desktop. Every part designed and 3D printed from Rhino models (except tubing and pump), then sanded, filled with bondo and filler primer, and finished in matte black.
